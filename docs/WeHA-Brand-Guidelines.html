01 — Brand Identity

Who WeHA is.

WeHA — We Help Automate — is an AI process automation partner for small and mid-sized businesses. We map the manual work that quietly drains a team, then build automation that runs without them.

The essence

Everything below is a downstream expression of one idea: automation that removes work, not attention. WeHA is the calm, competent operator who shows up, understands the real problem, and quietly makes it disappear. Not a flashy tech vendor. Not a hype machine. A partner who does the work and tells you the truth about it.

Brand pillars

PillarWhat it means in practice
HonestWe give our real opinion, even when it costs us the sale. No inflated promises, no manufactured urgency, no pitch decks. "No BS" is a brand rule, not a tagline.
Outcome-ledWe talk about the result — hours saved, deals not dropped, work that runs itself — before we talk about the technology. The tool is never the headline.
Calm & preciseRestraint over noise. Whitespace over clutter. Confidence expressed through clarity, not volume. The visual system is quiet on purpose.
Approachable expertiseDeep capability, delivered in plain language. We never make the buyer feel behind. Warmth and competence in the same breath.

Positioning in one line

The line we protect We map your most time-consuming workflows, show you what's automatable today, and build one automation live — then give you our honest opinion. No BS.

The India signature

WeHA is built in India, serving businesses across the UAE, Australia, and Singapore. The Indian flag mark in the footer is a small, deliberate signal of craft and origin — never hidden, never over-stated. It waves gently; it does not shout.

02 — Logo System

The mark.

The WeHA wordmark is "The Cut" — We, a vertical violet stroke, and HA. The stroke is the brand's signature: a clean incision that separates "We" from "Help Automate," collapsed into a compact, confident mark.

WeHA

Construction

ElementSpecification
Wordmark typeGeneral Sans, 700 weight
Letter-spacing-0.045em
The stroke (the "cut")2–3px wide, height 1.4–1.45em, radius 1–2px, offset translateY(0.06em), accent violet
Stroke margin0 0.18em–0.2em
Case"We" and "HA" — mixed case exactly as shown. Never all-caps, never all-lower.

Animation states

The mark has three defined motion behaviours. They are part of the identity, not decoration.

StateBehaviour & where it's used
Morph introFull "We Help Automate" collapses into the compact "We | HA" as it scrolls into view. The extra letters shrink to zero while the violet stroke draws in from the top. Used once, on first reveal. 2.8s ease.
Seamless loopAfter the intro, the compact mark hands off to a gentle infinite loop — the stroke re-draws and "HA" eases in from the right, then pauses and repeats. 4.8s cycle.
Animated (footer)The continuous looping version used in the footer as a living brand signature. Pauses on hover.
Motion rule — read this The logo animation is deliberately exempt from prefers-reduced-motion. It is a small, essential brand mark and must animate consistently on every OS and browser. (Many Windows machines report "reduce" by default, which previously left the mark frozen.) Larger decorative effects elsewhere still respect reduced-motion — the logo does not.

Usage

Always
  • Keep the violet stroke at full accent color — it is the identity
  • Preserve the exact spacing and mixed case
  • Give it clear space of at least the height of the "H" on all sides
  • Use on the off-white base or the near-black dark base only
  • Let the mark animate — never ship a frozen version to save effort
Never
  • Recolor the stroke to anything but the accent violet
  • Add a gradient, shadow, outline, or glow to the wordmark
  • Stretch, condense, rotate, or re-space the letters
  • Place it on a busy photo or a low-contrast background
  • Recreate it in a different typeface
  • Spell it "Weha", "WEHA", or "weha" in the mark

The 90 / 8 / 2 rule Roughly 90% of any WeHA surface is neutral (canvas, surface, ink). About 8% is structure (borders, muted text). Only 2% is Ink Violet. If a screen looks purple, it's wrong — the accent earns its impact by being rare.

Accent discipline

Do
  • Reserve Ink Violet for the one action you want taken
  • Use the soft violet tint for focus states and gentle callouts
  • Switch to the lighter violet (#9b80e0) on dark backgrounds for AA contrast
  • Let neutrals carry the layout; let whitespace breathe
Don't
  • Use violet for large fills or full-bleed backgrounds
  • Introduce a second accent color (no teal, coral, blue, etc.)
  • Put two competing violet CTAs in the same viewport
  • Drop below WCAG AA contrast for body text on any surface

Historical note: WeHA briefly explored a coral (#f0552d) direction and a teal (#01696f) origin. Both were retired. The variable is still named --weha-teal for legacy reasons — the value is Ink Violet. Never reintroduce coral or teal.

05 — Motion Language

How the brand moves.

WeHA motion is calm, purposeful, and slow. Nothing bounces, nothing races. Movement exists to guide attention and signal life — never to perform. If an animation draws attention to itself, it's wrong.

Motion principles

PrincipleIn practice
Ease, don't snapStandard curve is a soft cubic-bezier (.5,.1,.2,1). Transitions land gently. No linear, no harsh springs.
Slow over fastUI transitions sit at 0.2–0.4s. Ambient loops run 3.6–5s. The pace is unhurried on purpose.
One thing at a timeReveals are staggered, not simultaneous. The eye is led, never flooded.
Respect the userLarge decorative motion honours prefers-reduced-motion. The logo mark is the single deliberate exception.

Voice in practice

WeHA says
We map your most time-consuming workflows and build one automation live on the call.
Not

WeHA says
No sales scripts. No pitch decks. Just a conversation about your workflow.
Not

WeHA says

Not

Hard writing rules

Always
  • Lead with the outcome, not the technology
  • Write like you'd talk to a smart friend who's busy
  • Use short sentences and real numbers (hours, days, deals)
  • Say "No BS" and mean it — honesty is the differentiator
  • Cut every word that isn't earning its place
Never
  • Use em-dashes in any customer-facing copy — ever
  • Manufacture urgency ("act now," "limited spots")
  • Stack buzzwords (synergy, revolutionary, seamless, next-gen)
  • Over-promise or imply guarantees we can't keep
  • Talk down to the reader or make them feel behind
The em-dash rule WeHA customer-facing copy contains zero em-dashes. Use a period, a comma, or restructure the sentence. This is non-negotiable and applies to the site, emails, social, and every asset.

Signature phrases

These are established WeHA lines. Reuse them for consistency; don't reinvent the wheel each time.

09 — Layout & Grid

Space & structure.

WeHA layouts breathe. Generous whitespace is not empty space — it's the confidence to let one thing matter at a time. Content lives on a calm, centered measure with clear vertical rhythm.

The spacing scale

Spacing steps in a consistent rhythm. Sections are separated by large vertical space; elements within them by smaller, proportional steps.

8
16
24
32
44
64
88
120

Base unit 8px. Section padding sits at 88px (desktop) / 60px (mobile). Content max-width holds around 1080px with 32px gutters.

Layout principles

PrincipleIn practice
One idea per bandEach full-width section makes a single point. Alternating canvas / surface bands create rhythm.
Generous guttersNever crowd the edges. Whitespace signals premium restraint.
Hairline structureDividers and card edges are thin 12%-ink borders, never heavy rules.
Left-aligned readingBody copy is left-aligned on a comfortable measure (~68ch). Center only short display moments.
Consistent radius14px cards, 10px inputs, 999px pills. No sharp corners anywhere in the UI.
Accessibility floor Body text meets WCAG AA contrast on every surface, light and dark. Inputs use 16px minimum text. Focus states are always visible (soft violet ring). Large decorative motion respects reduced-motion. These are requirements, not preferences.
10 — Standards & Governance

Keeping it WeHA.

A brand is only as strong as its consistency. This section is the checklist anyone should run before shipping something under the WeHA name.

The pre-ship checklist

✓Check
☐Ink Violet is the only accent, and it's used sparingly (the 90/8/2 rule)
☐Logo is the correct "We | HA" cut, with the violet stroke, unmodified
☐Display type is Instrument Serif; body is Plus Jakarta Sans
☐Zero em-dashes in any customer-facing copy
☐Copy leads with an outcome, sounds honest, skips the buzzwords
☐Corners are rounded (14 / 10 / 999), borders are hairline
☐Motion is calm and eased; large decorative motion respects reduced-motion
☐Contrast meets WCAG AA; focus states are visible
☐Any form surfaces its errors — no silent failures

Common misuse — catch these

Red flags
  • Coral or teal appearing anywhere (both retired)
  • A frozen, non-animating logo
  • Purple used as a background fill instead of an accent
  • Buzzword-heavy or urgency-driven copy
  • Em-dashes in captions or headlines
Green flags
  • A page that looks 90% neutral with a single violet action
  • Copy a busy founder would actually want to read
  • Motion you barely notice but subtly feel
  • Consistent radius, spacing, and hairline borders
  • The animated "We | HA" mark, alive in the footer
